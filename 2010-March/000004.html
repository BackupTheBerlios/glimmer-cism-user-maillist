Dear Aleksandr,

> 1) Does time step adjust automatically when spatial resolution
> changes? And vice versa - does changing cell sizes have an influence
> on temporal resolution? I ask this because they are supposed to be
> related with each other via transport equations.

You will need to set the timestep and spatial resolution separately: 
they do not automatically adjust in response to one another. Given the 
non-linear nature of the dynamics (in contrast to, say, linear 
advection), the timestep/resolution criteria for convergence/stability 
are not simple.

> 2) If I remember correctly, when in the process of coupling glimmer to
> climate model by means of glint, one should make the following call:
> call glint(ice_sheet,time,temp,precip,orog)
> What is meant by orography? Does this quantity include ice topography
> or is it just bed elevation?

orog is the global topography which is consistent with the global 
forcing data (i.e. that seen by the climate model, or used in the 
generation of reanalysis data, or whatever you're using). It does 
include ice thickness.
