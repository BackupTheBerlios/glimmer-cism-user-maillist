Calving is certainly one of the darker corners of Glimmer-CISM.

Ice is only calved through the marine margin. Option 4 removes all ice 
on a marginal node below a certain depth. If it doesn't do it then it's 
a bug :-(. Is there a large area of ice below your marine limit?

Option 2 might work better since that removes all ice below certain depth.

> 
> What assumptions do ice-sheet modelers use for the balance/ relaxation 
> of the present-day bedrock topography. I simply 'ingnored' the fact that 
> some regions are not in equilibrium. Are there reference maps available 
> how the equilibrium bedrock topography would look like for present day 
> ice-sheets (globally). What initial states do ice-sheet modelers use for 
> present-day ice-sheet simulations.
> 
> 
this is somewhat tricky, because the isostatic adjustment depends on the 
  ice history. I guess you could use a global loading history, e.g. Dick 
Peltier's ICE-5g to initialise the isostatic module. This would be quite 
a good addition to GC (any volunteers?). For my predictive runs (using a 
predecessor of Glimmer) I started the runs in the past and continued 
into the future. Again, you have the problem of what initial topography 
to use. However, if your simulated present day topo resembles the actual 
present day topo you can have some confidence that you are not too far off.
