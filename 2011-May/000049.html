Hi All,
 
When using glimmer to test the seaRISE experiment (Sea-level Response to Ice Sheet Evolution), I found the results unreasonable. I reported it here, and am wondering if  someone could give me some suggestions.
 
I ran the glimmer with glint compotent, totally following the seaRISE experiments. (All data are downloaded from the website---http://websrv.cs.umt.edu/isis/index.php/Future_Climate_Data). However, diffirent versions of glimmer showed diffirent stories with the same forcing.
 
For glimmer-1.7, the ice volume and ice area kept increasing from 2004 to 2098 significantly, but the temperature and precipitation both increases with time in the forcing data, indicating more ice in future?
 
For glimmer-CISM2 (latest version), the ice volume and ice area also kept increasing from 2004 to 2098. But the precipitation in the output was unchanged from 2004 to 2098 and the ice temperature and annual mean surface temperature in the output  showed a decreasing trend from 2004 to 2098 . Actually, the temperature and precipitation both increases with time in the forcing data. The model can not reflect the real forcing? (Dr.Jesse Johnson also found this problem )
 
For glimmer-CISM2(stable_1.6),  the ice volume and ice area exhibited little change. The ice temperature and annual mean surface temperature in the output also showed very little change(still decreasing). The precipitation in the output remian unchanged with time.
